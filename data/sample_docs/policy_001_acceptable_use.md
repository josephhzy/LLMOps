# POLICY-001: Acceptable Use of Information Technology Resources

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines the acceptable and prohibited uses of all organisation information technology resources. It establishes expectations for responsible behaviour, protects organisational assets, and ensures compliance with the organisation's internal governance standards and applicable data-protection regulations (e.g., GDPR and other industry-standard privacy frameworks).

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all individuals who access IT resources, including full-time employees, part-time staff, contractors, interns, consultants, and authorised third-party representatives. IT resources include, but are not limited to: workstations, laptops, mobile devices, email systems, internet access, cloud services, internal applications, printers, and network infrastructure.

## 3. Acceptable Use

IT resources are provided primarily for official business purposes. Users are expected to:

- Use systems in a professional manner consistent with their role and responsibilities
- Protect their credentials and never share passwords or security tokens with others
- Lock or log off workstations when unattended, even momentarily
- Report suspected security incidents or policy violations promptly to the IT Helpdesk or ISO
- Comply with all applicable data classification and handling procedures (see SOP-004)
- Use only approved software and services listed in the Approved Software Catalogue

## 4. Prohibited Activities

The following activities are strictly prohibited on organisation IT resources:

- Accessing, downloading, or distributing offensive, obscene, or illegal material
- Attempting to bypass security controls, firewalls, or access restrictions
- Installing unauthorised software, browser extensions, or hardware peripherals
- Using organisation systems to conduct personal business activities or private commercial ventures
- Sharing classified or restricted information through unapproved channels (e.g., personal email, consumer messaging apps, unauthorised cloud storage)
- Connecting personal devices to the internal network without Mobile Device Management (MDM) enrolment
- Running network scanning, penetration testing, or exploitation tools outside of authorised security assessments
- Cryptocurrency mining or any resource-intensive non-work computation

## 5. Monitoring Notice

All activity on organisation IT resources is subject to monitoring, logging, and auditing without prior notice. This includes email content, internet browsing history, file access patterns, application usage, and system commands. Monitoring is conducted for security, compliance, and operational purposes. By using organisation IT resources, users acknowledge and consent to such monitoring. Monitoring data is retained for a minimum of 12 months and access is restricted to the ISO and authorised audit personnel.

## 6. Limited Personal Use

Incidental personal use of IT resources is permitted provided it:

- Does not interfere with work duties or productivity
- Does not consume excessive bandwidth or storage
- Does not involve any prohibited activity listed in Section 4
- Is limited to break times where practicable
- Does not create any legal liability for the organisation

Personal use of corporate email for non-work correspondence should be minimal. Users must not use their corporate email address to register for personal online services or subscriptions.

## 7. Violations and Enforcement

Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action proportionate to the severity of the breach, including:

- **Minor violation** (first offence): Written warning and mandatory refresher training
- **Moderate violation**: Temporary suspension of IT access privileges (up to 30 days) and formal counselling
- **Serious violation**: Termination of employment or contract, and referral to the appropriate authorities if unlawful activity is suspected

All violations are recorded in the employee's personnel file. Repeat offences within a 12-month period are escalated by one severity level. The ISO maintains the authority to immediately revoke access privileges pending investigation when there is reasonable suspicion of a serious violation.
