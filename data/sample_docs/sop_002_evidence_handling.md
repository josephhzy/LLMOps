# SOP-002: Digital Evidence Handling and Chain of Custody

## 1. Purpose

This procedure defines the requirements for acquiring, authenticating, storing, and transferring digital evidence to ensure admissibility in legal proceedings and compliance with applicable rules of evidence and industry-standard forensic practice (e.g., ISO/IEC 27037). All digital forensic activities conducted by all personnel must follow this SOP without exception.

## 2. Scope

Applicable to all Evidence Examiners, Digital Forensics Analysts, and any authorised personnel who interact with digital evidence in support of internal investigations, incident response activities, or regulatory examinations.

## 3. Chain of Custody Requirements

A chain of custody record must be initiated the moment digital evidence is identified. The record must capture:

- **Exhibit reference number** (auto-generated by the Evidence Management System, EMS)
- **Date and time of seizure or acquisition** (UTC, 24-hour format)
- **Acquiring examiner's name, role, and division**
- **Location of seizure** (address, room, rack ID as applicable)
- **Description of item** (make, model, serial number, condition)
- **Reason for seizure** (case reference number)

Every subsequent transfer, access, or return must be logged in the EMS with the receiving party's acknowledgement. Gaps in the chain of custody may render evidence inadmissible.

## 4. Acquisition Procedures

### 4.1 Imaging

All storage media must be imaged using write-blocked hardware (e.g., Tableau T35689iu or equivalent). A bit-for-bit forensic image shall be created using validated tools such as FTK Imager, EnCase, or dd with appropriate flags. The original media must never be directly examined.

### 4.2 Volatile Data

For live systems, capture volatile data in the following order of priority: running processes, network connections, logged-in users, system time, RAM contents. Use the approved volatile data capture toolkit maintained by the Digital Forensics Unit.

### 4.3 Cloud and Remote Sources

For cloud-hosted evidence, coordinate acquisition through the Legal and Compliance Division. Preservation requests must be issued to service providers within 24 hours of identification. All API-based acquisitions must be logged with request and response hashes.

## 5. Hashing Requirements

Every forensic image must be verified with dual hashing:

- **SHA-256** (primary integrity check)
- **MD5** (secondary, for backward compatibility with legacy systems)

Hash values must be generated at the time of acquisition and re-verified before analysis, before transfer, and upon receipt by any new custodian. Any hash mismatch must be reported immediately to the Lead Examiner and documented as an integrity exception in the EMS.

## 6. Storage Requirements

Digital evidence must be stored in the Evidence Storage Facility (ESF), which maintains access-controlled, climate-regulated, and CCTV-monitored conditions. Portable media shall be stored in tamper-evident bags with unique barcode identifiers. Logical evidence (images, extractions) shall reside on the air-gapped Forensic Evidence Server with AES-256 encryption at rest. Retention periods follow the Evidence Retention Schedule published by the Legal Division, with a default minimum of 7 years for matters with potential legal exposure.

## 7. Handling Logs

All interactions with evidence -- including viewing, copying, analysing, or transporting -- must be recorded in the Evidence Handling Log within the EMS. Entries are immutable once submitted. Quarterly audits of handling logs are conducted by the Quality Assurance Unit to verify compliance with this SOP.
