# GUIDE-001: New Analyst Onboarding Checklist and Procedures

## 1. Purpose

This guide provides a standardised onboarding process for new analysts joining the Technical Departments. It ensures that all new hires receive consistent access provisioning, mandatory training, and orientation to organisational standards within their first 90 days.

## 2. Scope

This guide applies to all new analysts (including lateral transfers and inter-department moves) assigned to the Engineering, Cybersecurity, or Data Analytics divisions. Hiring managers and assigned mentors share responsibility for ensuring completion of all onboarding milestones.

## 3. Pre-Arrival Setup (HR and IT Operations)

The following must be completed before the analyst's first day:

- [ ] Workstation provisioned with standard build and required software
- [ ] Network account created with Viewer-level RBAC role (see SOP-003)
- [ ] Email account and calendar access configured
- [ ] Building access card programmed for assigned floor and common areas
- [ ] Secure storage locker assigned for personal effects and classified materials
- [ ] Welcome pack distributed (employee handbook, organisation chart, IT quick-start guide)

## 4. Week 1: Orientation and Account Setup

| Day | Activity | Owner |
|-----|----------|-------|
| Day 1 | Division welcome briefing and facility tour | Hiring Manager |
| Day 1 | IT account activation and MFA enrolment (hardware key + TOTP) | IT Helpdesk |
| Day 1 | Collect and sign Acceptable Use Policy acknowledgement (Policy-001) | HR |
| Day 2 | Introduction to Incident Management System and ticketing workflows | Buddy/Mentor |
| Day 3 | Walkthrough of data classification tiers and handling procedures (SOP-004) | Mentor |
| Day 4 | Access request submission for division-specific tools and databases | New Analyst |
| Day 5 | End-of-week check-in with Hiring Manager | Hiring Manager |

## 5. Required Training (Weeks 2-4)

All training must be completed within 30 calendar days of start date. Failure to complete mandatory training results in access suspension until remediated.

1. **Cybersecurity Awareness Fundamentals** (e-learning, 4 hours) -- Covers phishing recognition, password hygiene, social engineering, and reporting procedures.
2. **Data Classification and Handling** (instructor-led, 2 hours) -- Practical workshop on applying SOP-004 to daily tasks.
3. **Evidence Handling and Chain of Custody** (instructor-led, 3 hours) -- Required for Engineering and Cybersecurity divisions. Covers SOP-002 procedures with hands-on lab exercises.
4. **Incident Response Procedures** (e-learning, 2 hours) -- Overview of SOP-001 including severity classification and escalation matrix.
5. **Workplace Safety and Emergency Evacuation** (in-person, 1 hour) -- Mandatory for all employees.

Training completion records are maintained in the Learning Management System (LMS) and are auditable.

## 6. Mentor Assignment

Each new analyst is assigned a mentor from within their division. The mentor must hold at least a Senior Analyst role and have a minimum of 2 years within the organisation. Mentor responsibilities include:

- Weekly 30-minute check-ins during the first 90 days
- Guidance on division-specific tools, processes, and unwritten norms
- Providing constructive feedback to the Hiring Manager at the 30-day and 60-day marks
- Serving as the first point of contact for procedural questions

Mentor assignments are coordinated by the Division's People Development Lead and recorded in the HR system.

## 7. Probation Review

All new analysts undergo a 6-month probation period. Formal reviews are conducted at:

- **3-month review**: Assess training completion, system proficiency, and cultural fit. Conducted jointly by the Hiring Manager and Mentor. Any concerns must be documented with a remediation plan.
- **6-month review**: Final probation assessment evaluating overall performance, adherence to SOPs, and teamwork. Successful completion results in confirmation of permanent appointment. If performance is unsatisfactory, probation may be extended by up to 3 months (once only) or employment may be terminated.

Review outcomes are recorded in the HR Performance Management System and shared with the Division Director for endorsement.
